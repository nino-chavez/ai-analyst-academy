# Leadership Track Certification

> **Track:** Leadership (Phases 5-6)
> **Credential:** AI Analyst — Leader
> **Prerequisite:** Practitioner Track

---

## Overview

The Leadership Track validates your ability to **lead organizational AI transformation**. You can sell AI to skeptics, build capable teams, develop enterprise strategy, and design operating models for sustained AI success.

---

## Required Deliverables

### 1. AI Initiative Proposal (Phase 5)

A complete proposal for a significant AI initiative, including influence strategy.

**Required Elements:**

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Executive Pitch** | Compelling case for the initiative |
| **Stakeholder Map** | Power/interest analysis with strategies |
| **Team Capability Assessment** | Current vs. required capabilities |
| **Project Scope** | Bounded scope with success criteria |
| **Objection Playbook** | Anticipated objections with responses |
| **Communication Plan** | How to keep stakeholders engaged |

**Rubric:**

| Criteria | 1 (Beginning) | 2 (Developing) | 3 (Proficient) | 4 (Exemplary) |
|----------|---------------|----------------|----------------|---------------|
| **Executive Pitch Quality** | Weak or unconvincing | Basic structure | Compelling with evidence | Would fund based on this |
| **Stakeholder Sophistication** | Generic analysis | Basic mapping | Strategic with tactics | Political mastery |
| **Team Assessment** | Ignored or vague | Basic gap analysis | Actionable capability plan | Development roadmap with ROI |
| **Scope Definition** | Too broad or vague | Reasonable bounds | Well-defined with criteria | Expert scoping with contingencies |
| **Objection Handling** | Not addressed | Generic responses | Tailored to stakeholders | Anticipated objections I hadn't thought of |
| **Communication Strategy** | Ad hoc | Basic plan | Systematic with cadence | Best-practice change management |

**Minimum to pass:** 3.0 average

---

### 2. Enterprise AI Roadmap (Phase 6)

A strategic roadmap for AI adoption across an organization or division.

**Required Elements:**

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Discovery Analysis** | AEO and search disruption considerations |
| **Vertical Strategy** | Industry-specific AI playbook |
| **Portfolio Prioritization** | Scored initiatives with sequencing |
| **Operating Model Design** | Governance and organizational structure |
| **Capability Building Plan** | How the organization will develop AI skills |
| **Multi-Year Roadmap** | Phased transformation plan |

**Rubric:**

| Criteria | 1 (Beginning) | 2 (Developing) | 3 (Proficient) | 4 (Exemplary) |
|----------|---------------|----------------|----------------|---------------|
| **Discovery/AEO Strategy** | Not addressed | Mentioned | Thoughtful analysis | Innovative approach |
| **Industry Relevance** | Generic | Some tailoring | Clearly industry-specific | Deep vertical expertise |
| **Portfolio Rigor** | No prioritization | Basic ranking | Scored framework | Multi-criteria with dependencies |
| **Operating Model** | Not addressed | Basic structure | Complete framework | Innovative, contextual design |
| **Capability Strategy** | Ignored | Basic training plan | Comprehensive development | Culture transformation |
| **Roadmap Quality** | Unrealistic | Plausible | Professional-grade | Could present to board |

**Minimum to pass:** 3.0 average

## Self-Assessment Checklist

Before submitting, verify:

### AI Initiative Proposal

- [ ] An executive would find this pitch compelling
- [ ] Stakeholder strategies are specific, not generic
- [ ] Team capabilities are honestly assessed
- [ ] Scope is achievable, not aspirational
- [ ] I've anticipated the real objections
- [ ] Communication plan sustains momentum

### Enterprise AI Roadmap

- [ ] Discovery/AEO strategy is forward-looking
- [ ] Vertical strategy reflects real industry dynamics
- [ ] Portfolio is prioritized with clear criteria
- [ ] Operating model fits the organization's culture
- [ ] Capability plan is actionable
- [ ] A board would take this seriously
