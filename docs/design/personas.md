# AI Operator Academy — User Personas

## Overview

This document defines the four primary user personas for the AI Operator Academy. Each persona represents a distinct user segment with unique backgrounds, goals, pain points, and optimal learning paths through the curriculum.

---

## Persona 1: The Career Pivoter

### Profile

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Name** | Sarah Chen |
| **Age** | 34 |
| **Role** | Marketing Operations Manager |
| **Industry** | B2B SaaS |
| **Tech Comfort** | Intermediate (uses Salesforce, HubSpot, Excel) |
| **AI Experience** | Basic ChatGPT user, no systematic knowledge |

### Background

Sarah has 10+ years in marketing operations. She's watched AI transform her industry and sees colleagues using AI tools, but feels she's "just scratching the surface." Her company is investing in AI initiatives, and she wants to be part of that conversation — not replaced by it.

### Goals

1. **Immediate**: Learn to use AI tools more effectively in daily work
2. **Medium-term**: Lead AI adoption initiatives on her team
3. **Long-term**: Position herself for AI-adjacent roles (Product Manager, Operations Strategy)

### Pain Points

- "I don't know what I don't know about AI"
- "I use ChatGPT but feel like I'm not getting its full potential"
- "Technical AI courses assume coding knowledge I don't have"
- "I need practical skills, not theory"

### Motivations

- Career security and advancement
- Staying relevant in changing job market
- Being seen as innovative by leadership
- Concrete, applicable skills

### Learning Preferences

- Prefers structured, sequential learning
- Values real-world examples over abstract concepts
- Needs to see immediate applicability to her work
- Appreciates business context ("why this matters")
- Learns best through doing, not just reading

### Optimal Curriculum Path

```
Phase 1 (AI Literacy) → Phase 2 (Workflows) → Phase 3 (Agentic Orchestration) → Phase 4 (Strategy)
```

**Full sequential curriculum** — Sarah benefits from building foundational knowledge before advancing. She'll engage deeply with each module and complete all labs.

### Key Touchpoints

- **Onboarding**: Skill assessment to validate starting point
- **Engagement**: Daily goal reminders, streak tracking
- **Support**: Scaffolded exercises with hints available
- **Motivation**: Progress milestones tied to career outcomes

### Success Metrics

- Completes full curriculum within 10 weeks
- Builds portfolio of 4 deliverables
- Achieves "Proficient" level in at least 3 competency areas
- Exports portfolio for LinkedIn/job applications

## Persona 2: The Business Student

### Profile

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Name** | Marcus Johnson |
| **Age** | 22 |
| **Role** | Senior, Business Administration Major |
| **Institution** | State University |
| **Tech Comfort** | Digital native, comfortable with tools |
| **AI Experience** | Uses AI for schoolwork, interested in applications |

### Background

Marcus is graduating in 6 months and sees "AI skills" in every job posting. He's used ChatGPT for essays and research but knows that's not what employers mean. He wants to differentiate himself from other business graduates and build a portfolio that demonstrates practical AI capability.

### Goals

1. **Immediate**: Build portfolio pieces for job applications
2. **Medium-term**: Land an entry-level role in AI-adjacent field
3. **Long-term**: Career in Product Management or Strategy

### Pain Points

- "Every job wants AI experience but how do I get it without a job?"
- "I don't have real business processes to automate"
- "Business school doesn't teach practical AI skills"
- "I need something concrete to show employers"

### Motivations

- Competitive advantage in job market
- Building demonstrable skills (not just certificates)
- Interest in how AI changes business
- Desire to stand out from peers

### Learning Preferences

- Can handle faster pace than working professionals
- Values portfolio-building over theory
- Motivated by tangible outcomes (artifacts to show)
- Comfortable with self-directed learning
- Appreciates gamification and progress tracking

### Optimal Curriculum Path

```
Phase 1 (AI Literacy) → Phase 2 (Workflows) → Phase 3 (Agentic Orchestration) → Phase 4 (Strategy)
→ Capstone Project (heavy focus)
```

**Full curriculum with capstone emphasis** — Marcus should complete everything but invest extra time in the capstone project, which becomes his primary portfolio piece.

### Key Touchpoints

- **Onboarding**: Emphasize portfolio outcomes and job-readiness
- **Engagement**: Gamification, achievements, streak challenges
- **Support**: Peer community features (future enhancement)
- **Motivation**: Clear connection between deliverables and employability

### Success Metrics

- Completes curriculum within 8 weeks (accelerated pace)
- Achieves "Excellent" on at least 2 deliverables
- Produces portfolio-ready capstone project
- Can articulate AI skills in interview context

## Persona 3: The Team Lead

### Profile

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Name** | David Park |
| **Age** | 42 |
| **Role** | Director of Customer Success |
| **Industry** | Enterprise Software |
| **Tech Comfort** | High-level understanding, delegates technical work |
| **AI Experience** | Evaluates AI tools, doesn't implement them |

### Background

David manages a team of 15 customer success managers. He's being asked by executives to "find AI opportunities" but doesn't know where to start. He needs to make buy/build decisions about AI tools and wants to avoid expensive mistakes. He's time-constrained and needs to learn strategically.

### Goals

1. **Immediate**: Identify AI opportunities for his team
2. **Medium-term**: Make informed buy vs. build decisions
3. **Long-term**: Lead AI transformation for his department

### Pain Points

- "Vendors promise AI everything but I can't evaluate claims"
- "I don't have time to become an AI expert"
- "I need to speak intelligently about AI with executives and technical teams"
- "I'm worried about making expensive mistakes"

### Motivations

- Making smart decisions for his team
- Not wasting company resources
- Being seen as forward-thinking leader
- Protecting his team from poor AI implementations

### Learning Preferences

- Needs to learn efficiently (time-constrained)
- Values strategic frameworks over hands-on implementation
- Wants to understand enough to evaluate, not implement
- Prefers executive summaries and decision frameworks
- Learns through case studies and scenarios

### Optimal Curriculum Path

```
Phase 4 (Strategy) → Phase 1 (AI Literacy) → Phase 2 (Workflows) → [Optional: Phase 3]
```

**Strategy-first path** — David should start with Phase 4 (build vs. buy, ROI, risk) to immediately address his pressing decisions. Then backfill foundational knowledge. Phase 3 (implementation) is optional depending on his role.

### Key Touchpoints

- **Onboarding**: Emphasize decision-making frameworks and time efficiency
- **Engagement**: Executive summaries, key takeaways highlighted
- **Support**: Scenario-based exercises relevant to management decisions
- **Motivation**: Connect learning to real decisions he needs to make

### Success Metrics

- Completes Phase 4 and Phase 1 within 4 weeks
- Can evaluate AI vendor claims with confidence
- Produces strategic recommendation document
- Makes informed buy/build decision for at least one initiative

## Persona 4: The Curious Explorer

### Profile

| Attribute | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Name** | Jamie Rodriguez |
| **Age** | 28 |
| **Role** | Freelance Graphic Designer |
| **Industry** | Creative Services |
| **Tech Comfort** | Variable (expert in creative tools, less in business tools) |
| **AI Experience** | Uses Midjourney/DALL-E, curious about broader applications |

### Background

Jamie is self-employed and uses AI image generation daily. They've heard about AI for writing, research, and automation but haven't explored systematically. They're not sure if this curriculum is for them but are curious about what AI can do beyond creative tools.

### Goals

1. **Immediate**: Understand what AI can and can't do
2. **Medium-term**: Find AI tools that help their freelance business
3. **Long-term**: Maybe pivot into AI-related services for clients

### Pain Points

- "I don't know if AI is relevant to me beyond image generation"
- "Business courses feel too corporate for freelancers"
- "I want to explore without committing to a rigid curriculum"
- "I learn best by experimenting, not following instructions"

### Motivations

- Intellectual curiosity
- Finding competitive advantages for freelance work
- Exploring new career possibilities
- Understanding the AI hype vs. reality

### Learning Preferences

- Prefers exploration over structure
- Values hands-on experimentation
- May skip around modules based on interest
- Needs to see relevance to their specific situation
- Motivated by discovery and "aha moments"

### Optimal Curriculum Path

```
Sandbox (Free Exploration) → Curriculum (pick modules of interest) → [Optional: Labs]
```

**Sandbox-first, curiosity-driven** — Jamie should start in the AI Sandbox to experiment freely. The curriculum serves as a reference they dip into as questions arise. They may never complete the full curriculum but will gain value from targeted exploration.

### Key Touchpoints

- **Onboarding**: Minimal friction, quick path to Sandbox
- **Engagement**: Discovery-oriented, "what if" prompts
- **Support**: Links from Sandbox to relevant curriculum sections
- **Motivation**: Surfacing relevant modules based on Sandbox activity

### Success Metrics

- Explores Sandbox within first session
- Completes at least 3 modules of interest
- Returns to platform multiple times
- Finds at least one AI application for their freelance work

## Design Implications

### Onboarding Flow

1. **Role Selection**: User self-identifies with one of four personas
2. **Path Recommendation**: System suggests optimal curriculum path
3. **Goal Setting**: User sets learning goals aligned with persona
4. **Skill Assessment** (optional): Validate starting point for Career Pivoters/Students

### Navigation Patterns

- **Career Pivoter**: Linear navigation, "Next Module" prominent
- **Business Student**: Linear with gamification overlays, achievement tracking
- **Team Lead**: Module picker, executive summaries, skip-to-key-concepts
- **Curious Explorer**: Sandbox-first, contextual curriculum links

### Content Presentation

- **Career Pivoter**: Full WHY-WHAT-HOW structure, business context emphasized
- **Business Student**: Faster pace allowed, portfolio connections highlighted
- **Team Lead**: TL;DR sections, decision frameworks prominent
- **Curious Explorer**: Experiment-first, theory-on-demand

### Progress Tracking

- **Career Pivoter**: Detailed progress, competency mapping, review queue
- **Business Student**: Achievements, streaks, leaderboards (optional)
- **Team Lead**: Milestone completion, time invested
- **Curious Explorer**: Activity log, "you might like" recommendations
